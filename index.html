What Mammoth collects

Nothing. Mammoth does not collect, store, transmit, or sell any personal information. It has no analytics, no telemetry, and no tracking of any kind.

Where your data goes

Nowhere. Mammoth runs entirely inside your browser on your own device. It has no server to send data to, and it never communicates with us or any third party about your browsing.

What's stored on your device

Only your own settings — things like your allowlist and whether the blocker is paused. This stays in your browser's local storage and is never uploaded. You can clear it anytime by removing the extension.

Permissions Mammoth uses

  • Reading and blocking network requests, so it can stop ads and trackers before they load.
  • Access to the pages you visit, so it can apply blocking rules — this never leaves your device.
  • Local storage, to remember your settings between sessions.

Third parties

None. Mammoth shares nothing with anyone, because it has nothing to share.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the updated version will be posted here with a new date. Mammoth's promise will not change: it does not watch you.
